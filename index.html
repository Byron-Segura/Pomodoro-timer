About Pomodoro Technique

What is Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It uses a kitchen timer to break work into intervals, typically 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks.

Each interval is known as a pomodoro, from the Italian word for tomato, after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer Cirillo used as a university student. - Wikipedia

Why use Pomodoro Technique?

  • Significantly improve your performance.
  • Excellent method to organize yourself and learn to prioritize tasks.
  • It is compatible with other study methods.
  • Avoid fatigue and cause motivation to achieve goals.
